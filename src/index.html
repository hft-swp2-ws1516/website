The fact that you are currently reading this means that you have successfully managed to find a website that is certainly not like the others. Congratulations! If you are also terribly interested in the neatly visualized results of our fully-featured SSL/TLS scanner, hotcat.de is your website of choice.

Hotcat is the result of a project carried out in scope of one of the lectures of the Computer Science study programme at the University of Applied Sciences Stuttgart. Until now, there might be two questions that have popped up in your mind:

  • What exactly can Hotcat do for me?
  • Why was it named "Hotcat" in the first place? Such a weird name...

As these are indeed very valid questions, why don't we have a closer look? Let us start with the second one. "Hotcat" simply stands for:

  • HfT
  • Open Source
  • Tool for
  • Crawling the
  • Advancements in
  • TLS

This information also provides a hint concerning your first question: What our application does is basically to scan the SSL/TLS properties of the one million most visited web sites throughout the World Wide Web according to alexa.com. The acquired results are saved in a huge database and then visualized in various graphs based on the user's selections (yes, that's you!).

The following shall now give you a short overview of the functions and possibilities each menu point at the very top of this page provides you with.

  • Home: Leads you to the page you are currently viewing. Pretty intuitive, isn't it?
  • Universal Summary: Offers several sub points that give you the possibility to quickly and easily view high-level graphs based on pre-defined selections. As the different name of the list items suggest, each graph covers a certain topic. Give it a try!
  • Detailed Filter: In case the Universal Summary menu point doesn't suit your need for details, you should have a look at the Detailed Filter. Essentially, it provides you with the possibility to search for any combination of an arbitrary cipher string, a top level domain and an SSL/TLS protocol. Besides, a built-in comparison function enables you to save any number of generated graphs in order to save them for later viewing and comparing.
  • World Map: Gives you an overview of the number of hosts offering (or not offering) Perfect Forward Secrecy in the form of a beautiful grey-and-white world map that is clickable and zoomable.
  • Analyse Me: If you have been wondering for the entirety of your life what the SSL/TLS properties of your favorite web site are, you will probably enjoy the "Analyse Me" feature. It provides you with a text field in which you can enter the URL of that certain web site which is then scanned. Please keep in mind that it might take a short while to execute the scanning process.
